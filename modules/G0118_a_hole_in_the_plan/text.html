A Hole in the Plot
a low fantasy city adventure for low level characters
( with some space for low level ethics )

Background

The annual Festival of the Storm is being moved to the smaller city square after last year excessive amount of firework power destroyed nearly half of the church glass facade. (proposed solution of stop using warfare sized fireworks being promptly dismissed, wouldn't be a Festival of the Storm without a fair amount of thundering). The sudden disappearance of one of the Ol'Holescavators, prodigious dwarven contraptions able to dig holes even in the sturdiest rock, has slowed the installment of the scaffoldings from where the fires are thrown.
Meanwhile, the richest and most detestable lady in town is lamenting a strange type of paranormal activity... A dozen of days ago Vigo "Sneaky" Mordsen, half-orc exponent of the lowlife in the city of Wyefield, found a big doortrap concealed on the floor of a warehouse were he was working, apparently leading to the sewers. When he realized there was a direct route from there leading under the house of one of the richest families in town he contacted his partner in crime to come up with a plan. Ardont "The Panther" Olzas had then him resign from the place as caretaker of the warehouse to took a job among the laborers for the preparations of the festival. He was then able to quickly steal from the foreman's belt the keys securing the escavators' chains by night in order to forge a copy. Now the city guard is on the clock, looking for the missing contraption, while the thief guild, unaware of the plan in act, is worried by disappearance of two known troublemakers. Meanwhile the Vigo and Ardont have started a slow grinding work under Orvienza's mansion, with aid from some [manovalanza] found among the city's lowest. Slow but not slow enough to have lady orvienza's sensible ears catch glimpses of wailing from the cellars, leading the old and already prone to paranoia women to think of some sort of supernatural haunting.do It's up to the PCs to take sides.

A note

Long backgrounds and descriptions are intentionally avoided and no specific deity, reign or faction is mentioned. A Hole in the plot is a short adventure suitable for a small party of 2nd / 3rd level, either as intro to a city campaign or as a one session standoff. and you should be able to tailor it to your setting no big changes in content, just additions from your existing settings. ( Also, knowing entire family trees of fairies who dwelled in the sacred woods, before the city foundation by a small group of dwarven miners, will be of no use to the PCs while they search a for a gigantic miller in the sewers )

Hooks

  • The temple keepers want to be sure the festival will be kept far from them; having no excessive trust in the city guard capacities, they will contact the party (maybe they know the cleric) to arrange some private investigation.
  • An Olde Holescavator is worth 800 gold pieces, so the city council is offering a (hardly negotiable) reward of 300 for the restitution ( must be intact and working ). Maybe Hildisa knows one of the PCs and (placing small trust on her men) directly contact the party proposing the work. The reward will be halved if the machinery if found after the festival took place.
  • The local thief guild contacts the shadier character in the party , they are worried about the disappearance of the Panther, they want to be sure he's not up to trouble. They offer a reward of 200 gold pieces for the retrieval. ( Should they come to know of the stolen excavator they are willing to pay for it 400 gold pieces )
  • Fruda Orvienza insistently asks help from the temple, lamenting the presence of a "day ghost" haunting her house; strange rumors are heard and she once found a couple of bottles shattered in the cellar. The temple is not taking her seriously but since the family contributed to last year reconstruction of the glass wall they ask the party to go and take a look.
  • The most charismatic character can know a young servant from the house who tells him/her about Lady Orvienza's problem, adding that Tino Orvienza is so annoyed by the wife perpetual lamentations that is offering a 100 gold pieces reward to anyone able to put an end to it.

Guards and Thieves

Every time PCs make a check involving the investigation ( rumours, perceptions etc. ) roll 1d6. On 1 they have been spotted by the opposing party:

- While working for the thieves guild, a guard patrol will stop them and start asking why they are interested in the theft, a persuasion check ( DC 15 ) is needed to remove suspects (later checks get a cumulative -4). Failing the check, the guards will bring the party to Fruda, where they'll be given a choice between imprisonment and double-crossing the guild.

- While working for the guards, a gang of thieves will "dissuade" the party from further investigation, the second time they are caught a fight will incur, unless a persuasion or intimidation ( DC 20 ) is made.

Both antagonist parties will fight trying not to do lethal damage, thieves will leave the PC unconscious and rob them of every non-weapon item carried (unless they recognise a weapon as masterwork or better) while guards will still bring PC to centrale for interrogation.
